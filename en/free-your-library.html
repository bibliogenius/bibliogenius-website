Free your library

Your books shouldn't be trapped on one platform.

BiblioGenius is designed to respect your digital sovereignty. Here's how to retrieve your data from the most common platforms to import them at home.

Watch video tutorial

📱 Gleeph

Export your library in CSV format:

  • Open the Gleeph app on your mobile.
  • Go to the Profile tab.
  • Tap the gear icon (Settings) at the top right.
  • Select Export my data.
  • You will receive a CSV file by email or can save it directly.

📖 Babelio

Retrieve your reading list:

  • Log in to your account on the Babelio website.
  • Go to My books.
  • Look for the Export my books link (usually at the bottom of the page or in list options).
  • Download the generated CSV file.

🌎 Goodreads

The standard Goodreads export:

  • Go to Goodreads.com and log in.
  • Click on My Books in the navigation bar.
  • In the left menu, click on Import and Export.
  • Click the Export Library button at the top right.
  • Wait for the file to be generated and click the download link.

📦 inventaire.io

The data-friendly sibling project:

  • Log in to inventaire.io.
  • Go to your profile Settings.
  • Look for the Data or Export section.
  • Choose CSV or JSON format (BiblioGenius prefers CSV for now).

What's next?

Once you have your file, open BiblioGenius and use the migration wizard to import your books in one click.
